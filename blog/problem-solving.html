Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes:

Pseudocode: 4/10

I understand why pseudocode would be a great tool for breaking down big steps/ projects. However, I really struggled to use it because I couldn’t understand what the task(s) was asking me to do. My friend has suggested that I should go back through old challenges and write pseudocode for things I have already completed. I think this could really help get better at using it as a tool.

Trying something: 10/10

I did a lot of this! I began to record every attempt that I made. This helped me keep track of what I had tried, to prevent myself from going around in circles. I also reached out to friends and they would explain what my attempt was doing. Sometimes this helped me understand what I needed to change to get the desired outcome.

Rubber ducky method 4/10

I’m not sure if the rubber ducky method really helped me during this sprint, however, as a visual person, I tried drawing what I thought the code might look like and found it easier to visualize what the code needed to do.

Reading error messages: 5/10

I find the small error symbols from visual studio to be helpful to a degree, however the test error messages went right over my head half the time. I think developing my fundamental understanding of javascript will improve this over time.

Console.logging: 5/10

I like console logging, and I think I will get better at using this tool as I use it more often. I do find it difficult to use it as a problem solving tool, as my knowledge is too limited to do much with the information that I get out of it.

Googling: 7/10

Googling was hit and miss for me this sprint. I find it very useful for basic foundational knowledge, but when it comes to combining multiple elements, I couldn’t find the information I was after. At least I was able to find good content to go over some foundations again.

Asking your peers for help: 9/10

I have a really strong group of friends who are always down to help and I feel safe asking them for assistance. I am extremely grateful for their time, and how they break down problems into bite sized pieces, without directly giving me the answer.

Asking coaches for help 6/10.

I found that I needed to step out of my comfort zone to ask for help from a coach, especially since I am more comfortable feeling vulnerable around my friends. However I also believe that not reaching out to my tutor was wasting a valuable resource.

Improving your process with reflection: 5/10

I find it hard to reflect when I haven’t achieved the outcome I am looking for. It’s easier when I can walk back through my steps and think about streamlining processes, rather than reflecting when I’m lost and don’t know what the next step should be. Hopefully I will find some good prompts that help me when I am lost.

Reflect on a time you were reluctant to ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differently next time?

I was originally reluctant to ask for help from a coach/tutor because I felt like I didn’t have a specific issue to ask about. I couldn't articulate how lost I felt with all of the tech content and I didn't want to waste my tutors time. Next time I may try to focus on one goal to achieve with help from my tutor.
