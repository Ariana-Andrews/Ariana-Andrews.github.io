Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence is the ability to understand, acknowledge and control one's emotions. It is also the ability to consciously recognise/understand and influence the emotion of others around you. Goleman's five-part model depicts five constituents of EQ:

  • Self-awareness
  • Self-regulation
  • Motivation
  • Empathy
  • Social Skills

How is EQ different from IQ?

EQ

  • Has five domains, as mentioned above.
  • Understanding, noticing and regulating emotions
  • Influenced by social and environmental factors, can be trained and developed over time
  • Important for general well-being
  • Can improve workplace productivity by creating constructive relationships and better group outcomes

Why is EQ important?

Emotional intelligence is the foundation for a healthy work/ life environment. Having higher EQ allows you to manage your emotions and make thoughtful decisions, rather than reactive decisions. This can increase your productivity during individual tasks and group projects.
