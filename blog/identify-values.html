Identify Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

One evening I decided to call the police on my neighbors because they were fighting. I felt very uncomfortable and it took a while for me to think about it before I acted because I knew it’d be obvious that the call came from our house. The fight started off with aggressive yelling, but it wasn’t until I heard punches that I decided I was morally obliged to do something to break up the conflict. I considered going over myself to try and meditate, but it was clear to me that they were drunk and I had to consider my own safety. I also wasn’t sure if I had enough conflict resolutions skills myself to actually make a positive impact on the situation. Part of me felt uncertain about stepping into someone else's business, even if it was a fairly intense confrontation to me, I didn’t know them very well. The final straw was when I realised I could hear the home owners child crying out, asking them to stop. In terms of values, I’d say that my compassion and drive to help protect the child from violence overpowered my fear of being confronted by the homeowner. I am unsure if I made the best decision, or whether I should have tried talking to my neighbor first. Maybe it would have been kinder to him if I had come out asking if he needed help, and called the police if things had started to escalate from there.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My parents shaped my compassion, kindness and desire to help others as well as my strong work ethic and tenacious tendencies. Whilst my partner's family increased my appreciation for family connection and work/life balance. My experience interacting with an online community of like-minded individuals has also deepened my appreciation for community and has pushed me to strengthen these connections both online and in-person. Growing up in Malaysia and traveling to nearby countries, after moving from a small NZ town, definitely opened my eyes to a wider world. It expanded my ideas of what was possible, formulated an understanding that humans have the potential to achieve so much more, and most importantly , exposed me to an even wider range of cultures, celebrations and communities. Without those experiences, I don’t think I would be the person I am today. I think I would have had a narrower outlook on life.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I’m consistent, persistent, and I don't like giving up. This will help me push through challenging learning modules and keep trying when things are tough. Even though I think I struggle with absorbing information sometimes, I think I have a strong tool kit to help me break things down, stay motivated and keep myself balanced as I study.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

After a lot of thought on this question, I’ve realised that a lot of the tensions over certain events that have arisen in my places of work have never really been addressed. At most I would communicate my need, for example, needing backup behind the bar sooner, and the manager would either implement it or not. If they didn’t I would adjust my mentality towards the situation and just do the best that I could with the resources at my disposal. Overall, this question has made me realise that I don’t have specific strategies for solving ‘tension’ when collaborating with someone and this is something I am looking to improve.
